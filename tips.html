The Five Rules of Cooking

First of all you must know the 5 Rules of Cooking. They are:

  1. Have a Good Knife and Cutting Board. Having 1 or 2 good knives (specially a chef's knife) is essential, along with a stable cutting board. If your cutting board starts slipping, put a wet paper towel underneath it. And most importantly, learn and practice knife skills to improve your speed and abilities in the kitchen.
  2. Prepare your Space and Ingredients before Hand. Before you start cooking, always clean and clear your space. Gather and prepare all the ingredients beforehand - this is a method used in professional kitchens called mise en place to stay organized and efficient.
  3. Cook a Lot. This tip isn't about cooking all the time, but about cooking in volume. Make more than needed now to save time later â€” freeze leftovers or repurpose them in other meals. It's efficient, convenient, and makes future meal planning easier.
  4. Cook with ingredients not only recipes. Beginner cooks often rely on recipes, but it's better to let fresh, quality ingredients guide your cooking. Instead of forcing a recipe with poor produce, choose what's fresh and in season, then decide what to make. Practice this weekly with at least one fruit or vegetable to build the habit.
  5. Master YOUR Foods Choose 5-10 recipes you and your family like, and make them regularly. Repeating dishes builds skill, speed, and confidence â€” and also gives you room to adapt them with seasonal ingredients or personal touches.
